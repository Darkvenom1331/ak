Masala Dosa

It is a popular South Indian breakfast snack not only in India but abroad as well. Though making masala dosa is a long process, but its worth it. In this recipe, the dosa is crisp and served with a delicious potato masala. If you do not have time, then skip making the potato masala and just make the sada dosa (plain dosa) with coconut chutney.

Ingredients:

1.1 cup rice
2.1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
3.1 tablespoon fenugreek seeds 4.Water, as needed 5.Salt, to taste 6.Oil or ghee, as needed

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

instructions

Instructions:

1.Rinse the rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds in water separately, and soak them in enough water overnight or for at least 6 hours.
2.Drain the water from the soaked rice, dal, and fenugreek seeds, and grind them together in a mixer or wet grinder to make a smooth batter. Add water as needed to get a slightly thick pouring consistency.
3.Transfer the batter to a large mixing bowl, and add salt to taste. Mix well with a spoon or clean hands. 4.Cover the bowl with a lid or cloth, and keep it in a warm place to ferment for 8 to 12 hours. The batter should rise and become slightly fluffy. 5.After fermentation, mix the batter well again. If it's too thick, add some water to get the desired consistency. 6.Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle over medium-high heat, and lightly grease it with oil or ghee. 7.Pour a ladleful of batter onto the center of the tawa, and spread it out into a thin circle using the back of the ladle in a circular motion. The dosa should be about 8-10 inches in diameter. 8.Cook the dosa for 1-2 minutes or until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy. 9.Flip the dosa using a spatula, and cook the other side for another minute or until it turns golden brown and crispy. 10.Serve hot with sambar, chutney, or any other side dish of your choice. 11.Enjoy your homemade dosa!
